Nuclear Medicine

03 Feb 2019
NORI Hospital

Department of Nuclear Medicine utilizes radioisotopes and radiopharmaceuticals for its state of the art diagnostic and therapeutic services. The department has acquired excellence over the years through dedication of specialized Nuclear Medicine Physicians and Nuclear Medicine Technologists. The radionuclide imaging and treatment protocols being used in the department are update according to latest international guidelines, which are constantly being refined with great attention for better patient service and minimizing the dose of radiation to which patients are exposed. The Department of Nuclear Medicine is a recognized post-graduate training centre for CPSP, PIEAS and other renowned institutes and is actively involved in research scenarios in nuclear medicine.

The Department is harboring Consultant and Specialist Nuclear Medicine Physicians having a high profile in the domains of diagnostic and therapeutic nuclear medicine. The department has to its credit, numerous publications in reputed international journals with a high impact factor. Consequent to provision of high quality services for patients, the number of patients visiting the department is on the rise. Considering this workload, the services are also being offered in the evening during the six working days.

Besides its significant role in maintaining the ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System of the AECH-NORI, the Department of Nuclear Medicine is the first IAEA-QUANUM (Quality Audits in Nuclear Medicine) Audited Department in Pakistan (second in Asia).

Radionuclide Gated-Ventriculography (MUGA)

Renal Scans

Renal imaging including DTPA Renal Scan and MAG-3 Renal Scan offer dynamic (a movie-like) assessment of function and excretion of both kidneys. This information cannot be obtained from other imaging modalities..
